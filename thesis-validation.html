Capture-Time Semantic Supervision for Humanoid Training

Capture-Time Semantic Supervision for

Humanoid Training

A short validation memo for robotics operators, founders, and embodied AI engineers.

We're exploring whether humanoid training pipelines are missing a data layer: capture-time semantic supervision.

The thesis is that raw teleoperation and video data may not be sufficient. Humanoids may benefit from task traces that capture scene structure, affordances, intent, contact/proprioception, and embodiment-specific kinematics at acquisition time rather than through post-hoc labeling.

If correct, this could transform teleoperation sessions into semantically structured embodied datasets, then use those captures to seed simulation amplification and policy training.

If incorrect, we want to understand why.
